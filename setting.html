The World of Pyrrhia

The Wings of Fire series takes place on Pyrrhia, a dragon-shaped continent divided into territories ruled by seven dragon tribes. Each tribe has adapted to their specific environment, developing unique abilities and societal structures. "The Dragonet Prophecy" introduces readers to this rich world through the eyes of five young dragons from different tribes.

Pyrrhia - The Continent

Pyrrhia is a large dragon-shaped continent composed of several different biomes. It is also known as the "Distant Kingdoms" by the dragons of Pantala (another continent introduced later in the series). Pyrrhia is the sole setting of the first ten books in the Wings of Fire series, including "The Dragonet Prophecy."

A large mountain range, the Claws of the Clouds Mountains, runs through the center of Pyrrhia, from the "wing" of Pyrrhia down to its "belly". The largest mountain in the range is Jade Mountain, which becomes significant in later books.

Major Geographical Features

  • Claws of the Clouds Mountains: Central mountain range where the dragonets were raised in a hidden cave
  • Diamond Spray River: Major river flowing through MudWing territory
  • Diamond Spray Delta: River delta where poorer MudWings live
  • Great Five-Tail River: River in the Kingdom of Sand
  • Bay of a Thousand Scales: Archipelago forming part of the SeaWing kingdom
  • Jade Mountain: Largest mountain in the Claws of the Clouds range
  • Scorpion Den: A lawless city in the Kingdom of Sand

The Seven Dragon Tribes

MudWings

Territory

The Mud Kingdom, which makes up the "back" of Pyrrhia. It consists of vast, marshy wetlands with stretches of grassland scattered with trees. Notable features include the Diamond Spray River and Diamond Spray Delta.

Government

Ruled by Queen Moorhen during the events of "The Dragonet Prophecy." MudWings have a less formal royal structure compared to other tribes, with more emphasis on sibling groups.

Alliances

Allied with Burn and the SkyWings in the War of SandWing Succession.

Physical Characteristics

  • Thick, armored scales in earthy colors (browns, amber)
  • Flat snouts with nostrils on top, allowing them to breathe while submerged
  • Thick, curved horns and broad, flat heads
  • Large, powerful bodies built for strength
  • May have gold or amber underscales

Special Abilities

  • Can hold breath for up to an hour
  • Can breathe fire if warm enough
  • Strong and muscular
  • Can camouflage in mud and dirt
  • Dragons hatched from blood-red eggs (like Clay) are immune to fire

Society & Culture

  • Live in sibling groups with the first-hatched (bigwings) as the leader
  • Less wealthy MudWings live near the Diamond Spray Delta
  • Live in large mud bunkhouses
  • No formal pairings; breeding night once a month
  • Parents don't raise their children; siblings form tight-knit groups
  • Eggs laid in protected bunkhouses with mud walls and warm rocks

Notable MudWings in Book 1

  • Clay - One of the dragonets of destiny
  • Asha - Member of the Talons of Peace who brought Clay's egg (appears only in prologue)

SandWings

Territory

Kingdom of Sand, which makes up the "chest" or southwestern portion of Pyrrhia. It consists of vast, scorching desert landscape with rolling dunes, flat terrain, and numerous oases.

Government

In dispute during "The Dragonet Prophecy" following Queen Oasis's death. Her three daughters—Burn, Blister, and Blaze—are fighting for the throne, causing the War of SandWing Succession.

Physical Characteristics

  • Pale yellow, pale gold, white-gold, or light yellow-brown scales
  • Obsidian-black eyes
  • Ridge/sail running from head to tail
  • Barbed, venomous tail like a scorpion's stinger
  • Black forked tongue
  • Short, curved horns

Special Abilities

  • Venomous barbed tails (cure is brightsting cactus)
  • Can survive long periods without water
  • Scales radiate heat
  • Can breathe fire
  • Can camouflage with sand
  • Adapted to desert conditions

Society & Culture

  • Many live in towns by oases
  • Some live and roam alone in the desert
  • Many work as merchants or assassins
  • Celebrate full moon festivals
  • Surprisingly musical tribe
  • During the war, most SandWings supported Blaze

Notable SandWings in Book 1

  • Sunny - One of the dragonets of destiny (later revealed to be half NightWing)
  • Dune - One of the guardians (missing a wing and tail barb)
  • Burn - Eldest daughter of Queen Oasis, allied with SkyWings and MudWings
  • Blister - Middle daughter of Queen Oasis, allied with SeaWings and secretly NightWings
  • Blaze - Youngest daughter of Queen Oasis, allied with IceWings (mentioned but doesn't appear)

SeaWings

Territory

Kingdom of the Sea, which forms the "tail" of Pyrrhia. It consists of small islands forming the Bay of a Thousand Scales archipelago, vast ocean stretches, coral reefs, and kelp forests.

Government

Ruled by Queen Coral, who is Tsunami's mother. The SeaWing royal family has a strong matriarchal tradition.

Alliances

Allied with Blister in the War of SandWing Succession.

Physical Characteristics

  • Blue, green, or aquamarine scales
  • Webbed talons
  • Gills on their necks
  • Powerful tails for swimming
  • Bioluminescent stripes on their bodies
  • Long, curved horns
  • Webbed spines along neck to tail tip

Special Abilities

  • Can breathe underwater through gills
  • Powerful swimmers
  • Exceptional night vision
  • Bioluminescent stripes for underwater communication (Aquatic language)

Society & Culture

  • Royal family has special spiral and starburst patterns on wings
  • Animus magic present in royal bloodline
  • Multiple palaces: Summer Palace (hidden in rainforest), Deep Palace (underwater), Island Palace (abandoned)
  • Speak both Aquatic (underwater light-based language) and Dragon (spoken language)
  • Strong emphasis on family connections, especially in royal line

Notable SeaWings in Book 1

  • Tsunami - One of the dragonets of destiny, daughter of Queen Coral
  • Webs - One of the guardians, former SeaWing soldier who stole Tsunami's egg
  • Queen Coral - Ruler of the SeaWings and Tsunami's mother
  • Anemone - Tsunami's younger sister, an animus dragon
  • Auklet - Tsunami's newly hatched sister
  • Commander Shark - Queen Coral's brother and army commander

RainWings

Territory

Rainforest Kingdom, which forms the "hind leg" or southeastern section of Pyrrhia. It consists of giant trees, vibrant flowers, tangles of vines, and thick canopies.

Government

During "The Dragonet Prophecy," RainWings have a rotation of queens, with each queen serving for a month. This changes later when Glory becomes queen.

Alliances

Not involved in the War of SandWing Succession. Most other tribes consider RainWings lazy and unintelligent, not worth forming alliances with.

Physical Characteristics

  • Scales that can change color at will
  • Prehensile tails for swinging through trees
  • Ruffs behind ears
  • Curved horns
  • Long, slender bodies
  • Usually prefer bright, vibrant colors when not camouflaging

Special Abilities

  • Can change scale color at will (camouflage)
  • Colors often reflect emotions
  • Can shoot venom from fangs that dissolves organic material
  • Prehensile tails for swinging through trees

Society & Culture

  • Generally peaceful and non-aggressive
  • Practice "sun-time" daily for energy and scale health
  • Primarily vegetarian
  • Keep sloths as pets
  • Rotating monarchy with multiple queens
  • Little knowledge of the outside world or the war

Notable RainWings in Book 1

  • Glory - One of the dragonets of destiny, a replacement for the SkyWing egg

NightWings

Territory

During "The Dragonet Prophecy," NightWings live on a volcanic island hidden from other tribes. Originally, they came from the lost city of night on the Talon Peninsula.

Government

Ruled by Queen Battlewinner, though this isn't revealed until later books. The tribe is secretive about their leadership structure.

Alliances

Secretly allied with Blister in the War of SandWing Succession, though they publicly claim to be neutral.

Physical Characteristics

  • Dark scales (black, dark green, blue, purple, or dull gray)
  • Silver scales scattered across wings like stars
  • Forked, pitch-black tongues
  • Mind readers have silver teardrop scales by corners of eyes

Special Abilities

  • Can blend into shadows and night sky
  • Breathe fire
  • Strong sense of smell and powerful night vision
  • Claim to have mind-reading and prophetic abilities (mostly false)
  • Hatched under one full moon: mind-reading OR prophecy
  • Hatched under two full moons: both mind-reading AND prophecy
  • Hatched under three full moons: stronger versions of both powers

Society & Culture

  • Secretive and mysterious
  • Other tribes fear and respect them for their supposed powers
  • Live on a volcanic island in harsh conditions
  • Value knowledge and scrolls
  • Manipulative, using their reputation to influence other tribes

Notable NightWings in Book 1

  • Starflight - One of the dragonets of destiny
  • Morrowseer - Delivered the Dragonet Prophecy, manipulates events throughout the book

SkyWings

Territory

Sky Kingdom, which forms the "wing" of Pyrrhia. It consists of cliffs, mountains, rivers, lakes, forests, and valleys.

Government

Ruled by Queen Scarlet during "The Dragonet Prophecy." SkyWings have a traditional monarchy with power passing to whoever kills the previous queen.

Alliances

Allied with Burn in the War of SandWing Succession.

Physical Characteristics

  • Red, crimson, orange, or yellow rust-colored scales
  • Yellow, orange, or amber eyes
  • Largest wings of any tribe
  • Long, curved horns
  • Thin, long snouts with nose-spike
  • Extra horn on jaw

Special Abilities

  • Fastest fliers with greatest endurance
  • Breathe fire
  • Powerful fighters
  • Better vision and cold resistance than most tribes
  • Can hold fire until maximum temperature

Society & Culture

  • Often portrayed as short-tempered and militaristic
  • Value entertainment (arena combat under Queen Scarlet)
  • Have orchestras for music during celebrations
  • Kill dragons with dangerous differences (animus, firescales)
  • Raise dragonets in a wingery

Notable SkyWings in Book 1

  • Queen Scarlet - Ruler of the SkyWings, captures and imprisons the dragonets
  • Peril - Arena champion with firescales, befriends Clay
  • Kestrel - One of the guardians, former SkyWing soldier

IceWings

Territory

Ice Kingdom, which forms the "head" of Pyrrhia. It consists of snowy and icy tundras, sub-zero temperatures, and freezing winds. The Great Ice Cliff (an animus-touched barrier) protects the northern border.

Government

Ruled by Queen Glacier during "The Dragonet Prophecy" (though she doesn't appear in the first book).

Alliances

Allied with Blaze in the War of SandWing Succession.

Physical Characteristics

  • Silvery-gray, white, pale blue, or pale purple scales
  • Dark blue, sky blue, icy blue, or stormy gray eyes
  • Forked blue tongues and dark blue blood
  • Diamond-shaped, narrow heads
  • Spikes down back and tail
  • Ruff of extra spikes behind heads
  • Serrated claws for gripping ice

Special Abilities

  • Can withstand subzero temperatures and bright light
  • Serrated claws for gripping ice
  • Sharper eyesight than other tribes
  • Scales emit icy-cold chill
  • Can exhale "frostbreath" (freezing weapon)

Society & Culture

  • Rigid social hierarchy with circle rankings
  • Formal, traditional, and strict
  • Value order and discipline
  • Natural enemies of NightWings

Notable IceWings in Book 1

  • Hvitur - Member of the Talons of Peace who appears only in the prologue
  • Fjord - Arena prisoner who fights Clay

The War of SandWing Succession

The backdrop of "The Dragonet Prophecy" is the War of SandWing Succession, a conflict that began when the SandWing queen, Queen Oasis, was killed by scavengers (humans) who stole treasure from her. Her three daughters—Burn, Blister, and Blaze—each claimed the throne, leading to a war that lasted 18 years by the time of the book's events.

Alliances in the War

  • Burn: Allied with SkyWings and MudWings
  • Blister: Allied with SeaWings and secretly with NightWings
  • Blaze: Allied with IceWings and most SandWings

Impact of the War

The war devastated Pyrrhia, with battles fought across the continent. Many dragons died, territories were destroyed, and resources depleted. The dragonets of destiny were raised specifically to end this war according to the prophecy, though they later discover the prophecy was fabricated by the NightWings.

The Talons of Peace

The Talons of Peace is a secret organization working to end the War of SandWing Succession. Members come from all dragon tribes and work together despite traditional tribal enmities. They were responsible for gathering the dragonets of destiny and raising them in secret to fulfill the prophecy.

Notable Members in Book 1

  • Kestrel (SkyWing): One of the guardians who raised the dragonets
  • Webs (SeaWing): One of the guardians who raised the dragonets
  • Dune (SandWing): One of the guardians who raised the dragonets
  • Asha (MudWing): Brought Clay's egg but died from battle wounds
  • Hvitur (IceWing): Killed by Burn while attempting to steal the SkyWing egg
  • Crocodile (MudWing): Later revealed to be a spy for Burn's forces

Human Settlements

Humans (called "scavengers" by dragons) exist in Pyrrhia but are not a dominant species. They live in scattered settlements and are often hunted by dragons. In "The Dragonet Prophecy," scavengers play a significant role as it was scavengers who killed Queen Oasis and stole her treasure, inadvertently starting the War of SandWing Succession.

During the arena fights in Queen Scarlet's palace, Clay and Tsunami are forced to fight scavengers. They notice that one of the scavengers is wearing jewelry that might have belonged to Queen Oasis.
