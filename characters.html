Characters in The Dragonet Prophecy

The first book in the Wings of Fire series introduces readers to a diverse cast of dragon characters from different tribes, each with unique personalities, abilities, and backgrounds. Below is a comprehensive list of all characters who appear in "The Dragonet Prophecy."

The Dragonets of Destiny

Clay (MudWing)

Physical Description

Clay has thick, brown scales the color of mud with amber and gold undertones. He's larger than the other dragonets, with a sturdy build typical of MudWings. His eyes are warm amber, and he has a flat snout with nostrils on top that allow him to breathe while mostly submerged.

Personality

Clay is loyal, protective, and often hungry. As a "bigwings" (first hatched in a clutch), he has natural protective instincts toward his friends, whom he considers his true siblings. He's generally peaceful and tries to avoid fighting when possible, though he will defend those he cares about without hesitation. Clay is also humble, kind-hearted, and sometimes naive.

Abilities

  • Fire breathing (when warm enough)
  • Immunity to fire (hatched from a blood-red egg)
  • Can hold his breath for up to an hour
  • Enhanced strength typical of MudWings
  • Natural fighting abilities

Background & Role

Clay was born from a blood-red egg purchased from his mother by the Talons of Peace. As the "wings of earth" in the prophecy, he was the first of the dragonets to hatch. Throughout the book, Clay serves as the main protagonist and the emotional center of the group. His journey involves discovering his true nature as a protector and learning about his tribe and biological family.

Key Relationships

  • Considers the other dragonets his true siblings
  • Forms a special friendship with Peril despite her deadly firescales
  • Has a complicated relationship with Kestrel, who was particularly harsh with him during training
  • Briefly meets his biological siblings but feels his true family is with the other dragonets

Character Development

Throughout the book, Clay grows from feeling inadequate and clumsy to embracing his protective nature. He discovers that what the guardians saw as weakness—his reluctance to fight and kill—is actually a strength. His immunity to fire, revealed during his fight with Peril, symbolizes his resilience and ability to withstand hardship while remaining kind and compassionate.

Tsunami (SeaWing)

Physical Description

Tsunami has deep blue scales that shimmer with hints of green. She has translucent green eyes and gills on her neck. Like all SeaWings, she has bioluminescent stripes on her body that can glow in the dark, webbed talons, and a powerful tail for swimming.

Personality

Tsunami is fierce, brave, and often impulsive. She has natural leadership qualities but can be headstrong and quick to anger. She's determined, independent, and not afraid to speak her mind or challenge authority. Despite her tough exterior, she cares deeply for her friends and will risk her life to protect them.

Abilities

  • Can breathe underwater through gills
  • Excellent swimmer
  • Can see in the dark
  • Bioluminescent scales for communication (Aquatic language)
  • Strong fighter

Background & Role

Tsunami is the daughter of Queen Coral, making her a SeaWing princess. Her egg was stolen from the royal hatchery by Webs, a member of the Talons of Peace. As the "wings of sea" in the prophecy, she was raised alongside the other dragonets. Tsunami is often the driving force behind the group's actions, pushing for escape and taking charge in dangerous situations.

Key Relationships

  • Has a complicated relationship with her mother, Queen Coral, upon meeting her
  • Forms a bond with her sister, Anemone
  • Feels betrayed by Webs for stealing her from her family
  • Often clashes with Glory due to their strong personalities but respects her

Character Development

Tsunami's arc involves her discovery of her royal heritage and the realization that her idealized vision of her home doesn't match reality. She learns to balance her impulsive nature with thoughtful leadership and discovers that family extends beyond blood relations to include the dragonets she was raised with.

Glory (RainWing)

Physical Description

Glory's scales can change color based on her emotions or at will for camouflage. Her natural colors tend toward vibrant purples, blues, and greens. She has a slender build typical of RainWings, with a prehensile tail and a ruff behind her ears.

Personality

Glory is intelligent, sarcastic, and determined. She has a sharp wit and often uses cutting remarks as a defense mechanism. Having been treated as a replacement and considered worthless by the guardians, she's developed a tough exterior and a strong desire to prove herself. Despite this, she cares deeply for the other dragonets and will risk herself to protect them.

Abilities

  • Can change the color of her scales at will
  • Can spit corrosive venom from her fangs
  • Prehensile tail for climbing and maneuvering in trees
  • Excellent camouflage abilities

Background & Role

Glory was not originally meant to be part of the prophecy. After the SkyWing egg was destroyed, Webs stole a RainWing egg to replace it. Because of this, Glory was often mistreated by the guardians, especially Kestrel, who considered RainWings lazy and useless. Throughout the book, Glory proves her worth repeatedly, saving the other dragonets multiple times with her quick thinking and unique abilities.

Key Relationships

  • Particularly close to Sunny, who was also often underestimated
  • Has a complicated relationship with the guardians, especially Kestrel
  • Develops a grudging respect for Tsunami despite their occasional clashes

Character Development

Glory's journey is about finding her place and proving her worth. From being considered a worthless replacement to becoming essential to the group's survival, she demonstrates that prejudice about RainWings is unfounded. Her discovery of her venom abilities and her eventual leadership role with her tribe show her growth from an angry, defensive dragonet to a confident leader.

Starflight (NightWing)

Physical Description

Starflight has black scales that sometimes appear dark purple in certain lights. He has a lithe build and silver scales scattered like stars under his wings. Unlike some NightWings, he doesn't have the silver teardrop scales by his eyes that would indicate mind-reading abilities.

Personality

Starflight is highly intelligent and scholarly, with a love for scrolls and knowledge. He's often anxious, cautious, and indecisive, preferring to think through all possibilities before acting. He can be pedantic and sometimes annoying to the others with his constant facts, but his knowledge frequently proves valuable. He's also deeply loyal to his friends, though sometimes conflicted about his tribe's expectations.

Abilities

  • Exceptional intelligence and memory
  • Can breathe fire
  • Can blend into shadows
  • Night vision

Background & Role

Starflight is the "wings of night" in the prophecy. His egg was given to the Talons of Peace by the NightWings. Throughout the book, Starflight struggles with his identity as a NightWing, especially when he meets Morrowseer and other NightWings who expect him to be loyal to their tribe above all else. He serves as the group's intellectual resource, often providing crucial information from scrolls he's read.

Key Relationships

  • Has a crush on Sunny that he tries to hide
  • Feels pressured by Morrowseer to be loyal to the NightWings
  • Often irritates Tsunami with his caution and indecision
  • Bonds with Clay over their shared love of food, though for different reasons

Character Development

Starflight's arc involves finding courage despite his fears and learning to trust his own judgment rather than blindly following authority or scrolls. He begins to question the NightWings' motives and eventually chooses loyalty to his friends over tribal expectations. His growth is about finding balance between knowledge and action, thought and feeling.

Sunny (SandWing/NightWing Hybrid)

Physical Description

Sunny is smaller than typical SandWings with golden scales rather than pale yellow. She lacks the venomous barb on her tail that is characteristic of SandWings. Her eyes are a striking green-gray color, and she has a more compact build than pure SandWings.

Personality

Sunny is optimistic, kind, and empathetic. She tries to see the best in everyone and believes strongly in the prophecy and their destiny to end the war. Often underestimated due to her small size and gentle nature, Sunny is actually quite clever and observant. She serves as the group's moral compass and emotional heart.

Abilities

  • Can survive in desert conditions
  • Scales radiate heat
  • Enhanced intuition and empathy
  • Can breathe fire

Background & Role

Sunny is the "wings of sand" in the prophecy. Her egg was taken from her mother, Thorn, by Dune, who was working with the Talons of Peace. Unknown to the guardians, Sunny is actually half NightWing, her father being an animus dragon named Stonemover. This explains her unusual appearance for a SandWing. Throughout the book, Sunny maintains faith in the prophecy and their ability to end the war, often serving as the peacemaker among the dragonets.

Key Relationships

  • Particularly close to Clay, who is protective of her
  • Forms a bond with Glory, as both were often underestimated
  • Unaware of Starflight's feelings for her
  • Has not yet met her biological parents in this book

Character Development

While Sunny's full character development extends beyond the first book, in "The Dragonet Prophecy" she begins to show hints of the strength beneath her sunny exterior. Despite being treated as the weakest link by the guardians, she demonstrates resilience and an unwavering moral compass. Her optimism in the face of adversity becomes a source of strength for the group.

The Guardians

Kestrel (SkyWing)

Physical Description

Kestrel is a large SkyWing with rust-colored scales and scars across her face and talons. She has orange-yellow eyes and a fierce appearance that matches her personality.

Personality

Harsh, strict, and often cruel, Kestrel is the most severe of the guardians. She believes in tough training and has little patience for weakness. Despite her rough exterior, she has a tragic past that explains some of her bitterness.

Background & Role

Kestrel was once a soldier in Queen Scarlet's army. She had twin dragonets: one with too much fire (Peril) and one with too little. Queen Scarlet forced her to kill the dragonet with too little fire and took Peril to raise as her champion. When Kestrel tried to escape with Peril, she was caught and scarred by her daughter's firescales. She joined the Talons of Peace and became one of the guardians raising the dragonets of destiny.

Webs (SeaWing)

Physical Description

Webs is an older SeaWing with blue-green scales that have faded with age. He has a gentle appearance compared to the other guardians.

Personality

Webs is the gentlest of the guardians, though still firm. He's more willing to bend rules than Kestrel or Dune, as evidenced by his decision to steal a RainWing egg to replace the destroyed SkyWing egg.

Background & Role

Webs was once a soldier in Queen Coral's army but defected to join the Talons of Peace. He stole Tsunami's egg from the royal hatchery and later stole Glory's egg from the rainforest when the SkyWing egg was destroyed. He taught the dragonets about history and geography.

Dune (SandWing)

Physical Description

Dune is a SandWing with pale yellow scales. He has a damaged wing that prevents him from flying and a scarred tail where his poisonous barb was cut off.

Personality

Quieter than the other guardians, Dune is stern but not as harsh as Kestrel. He has a particular interest in Sunny, though the reason isn't revealed until later books.

Background & Role

Dune was once a soldier in Burn's army but was maimed in battle, losing his ability to fly and his tail barb. He joined the Talons of Peace and was responsible for finding Sunny's egg. He taught the dragonets about hunting and survival skills.

SkyWing Kingdom Characters

Queen Scarlet

Physical Description

Scarlet has orange-gold scales and amber eyes. She wears jewelry made from the treasures of her defeated enemies and has a regal, intimidating presence.

Personality

Cruel, vain, and power-hungry, Queen Scarlet enjoys watching dragons fight to the death in her arena for entertainment. She's manipulative and sees other dragons as either tools to use or threats to eliminate.

Background & Role

Queen Scarlet is the ruler of the SkyWings and an ally of Burn in the SandWing succession war. She captures the dragonets and forces them to participate in her arena games. She has a particular interest in Clay due to his immunity to fire and his potential as a challenger to Peril.

Peril

Physical Description

Peril has unusually bright copper scales that seem to glow from the heat they generate. Her eyes are bright blue, standing out against her fiery scales. Smoke often rises from her scales when she touches most objects.

Personality

Despite her deadly abilities, Peril is socially awkward and lonely. Having been raised by Queen Scarlet to be her champion, she has little experience with normal dragon interaction. She's loyal to those who show her kindness and desperate for connection.

Abilities

  • Firescales - her scales are so hot they burn through anything she touches
  • Immunity to fire
  • Exceptional fighting skills from years in the arena

Background & Role

Peril was born with too much fire, a rare condition that makes her scales deadly to the touch. Her twin brother, born with too little fire, was killed at birth. Queen Scarlet raised Peril as her champion in the arena after forcing her mother to attempt an escape that resulted in her death. Peril forms a connection with Clay, who is immune to her firescales, and eventually helps the dragonets escape.

SandWing Characters

Burn

Physical Description

The largest and strongest of the three SandWing sisters, Burn has scales scarred from many battles. She has a particularly vicious appearance with cold, dark eyes.

Personality

Brutal, aggressive, and cruel, Burn enjoys collecting "weird" specimens—unusual dragons or animals that she kills and preserves. She's a formidable warrior who rules through fear and strength.

Background & Role

Burn is the eldest daughter of the late Queen Oasis and one of the three sisters fighting for the SandWing throne. She has allied with the SkyWings and MudWings in the war. She visits Queen Scarlet's palace and shows interest in the dragonets, particularly Sunny, whom she wants to add to her collection of oddities.

Blister

Physical Description

Blister is sleek and cunning-looking with night-black eyes. She's smaller than Burn but moves with calculated precision.

Personality

Intelligent, calculating, and manipulative, Blister is the most cunning of the three sisters. She prefers strategy over brute force and is willing to wait patiently for the right moment to strike.

Background & Role

Blister is the middle daughter of Queen Oasis. She has allied with the SeaWings and secretly with the NightWings in the war. She appears at the SeaWing palace when Tsunami and the other dragonets are there, attempting to manipulate them into choosing her as the SandWing queen.

Blaze

Physical Description

Blaze is described as beautiful with pristine scales and a charming appearance. She's the smallest of the three sisters.

Personality

Vain, shallow, and not particularly intelligent, Blaze is more concerned with her appearance than strategy or warfare. She's the least violent of the sisters but also the least capable as a potential ruler.

Background & Role

Blaze is the youngest daughter of Queen Oasis. She has allied with the IceWings in the war and controls the northern SandWing territory. She is mentioned but does not appear directly in the first book.

SeaWing Characters

Queen Coral

Physical Description

Queen Coral has pale blue scales with hints of pink near her wings and gills. She wears strings of pearls around her neck and horns.

Personality

Protective of her remaining daughters and passionate about writing, Queen Coral can be both loving and ruthless. She's intelligent and politically savvy but blinded by her love for her tribe and family.

Background & Role

Queen Coral is the ruler of the SeaWings and Tsunami's mother. She has lost many of her female heirs to a mysterious killer (later revealed to be Orca's enchanted statue). She allies with Blister in the SandWing succession war and welcomes Tsunami back to the kingdom, though she shows little interest in the other dragonets.

Anemone

Physical Description

Anemone has pale blue scales with hints of pink, similar to her mother. She's small for her age, as she's only about two years old.

Personality

Despite her young age, Anemone shows signs of intelligence and curiosity. She's been sheltered by her mother, kept literally tethered to her at all times for protection.

Abilities

  • Animus magic (revealed when she helps Tsunami fight Orca's statue)
  • Standard SeaWing abilities

Background & Role

Anemone is Tsunami's younger sister and one of the few surviving female heirs of Queen Coral. She is an animus dragon, though this is kept secret from most dragons. She helps Tsunami destroy the statue that has been killing the royal heirs.

Auklet

Physical Description

Auklet is a newly hatched dragonet with green scales.

Background & Role

Auklet is Queen Coral's newest daughter, whose egg Tsunami protects from Orca's statue. She hatches near the end of the book.

Commander Shark

Physical Description

Shark has blue-gray scales and cold eyes. He has a powerful build befitting his military position.

Personality

Stern, suspicious, and loyal to Queen Coral, Shark is distrustful of the dragonets and particularly hostile toward Tsunami.

Background & Role

Shark is Queen Coral's brother and the commander of her army. He is suspicious of Tsunami and the other dragonets, believing they might be assassins or spies.

Other Notable Characters

Morrowseer (NightWing)

Physical Description

Morrowseer is a massive NightWing with black scales and a commanding presence. He has cold eyes and a intimidating demeanor.

Personality

Calculating, manipulative, and mysterious, Morrowseer reveals little of his true intentions. He's willing to sacrifice others to achieve his goals and shows particular interest in Starflight as a fellow NightWing.

Background & Role

Morrowseer delivered the Dragonet Prophecy and occasionally checks on the dragonets' progress. He disapproves of Glory as a replacement for the SkyWing and orders her death. Later, he takes Starflight away to teach him about NightWing ways and expectations.

Hvitur (IceWing)

Physical Description

Hvitur had silvery-white scales typical of IceWings.

Background & Role

A member of the Talons of Peace who appears only in the prologue. He was killed by Burn while attempting to steal the SkyWing egg for the prophecy.

Asha (MudWing)

Background & Role

A member of the Talons of Peace who appears only in the prologue. She brought Clay's blood-red egg but died from battle wounds shortly after.
