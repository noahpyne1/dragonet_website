Book Summary

"The Dragonet Prophecy" is the first book in the Wings of Fire series written by Tui T. Sutherland. Published in 2012, it introduces readers to the world of Pyrrhia, a continent inhabited by seven dragon tribes caught in a devastating war. The story follows five young dragons—Clay, Tsunami, Glory, Starflight, and Sunny—who are raised in secret to fulfill a prophecy that claims they will end the war and choose the next SandWing queen.

The dragonets have been raised since hatching by the Talons of Peace, a secret organization working to end the War of SandWing Succession. After six years of training and living hidden in a mountain cave, the dragonets decide to escape their confining home and guardians. Their journey takes them across Pyrrhia, where they face numerous challenges including capture by the SkyWing queen, discovering secrets about their origins, and learning that the prophecy that has dictated their lives may not be what it seems.

Throughout their adventures, the dragonets form strong bonds with each other, discover their unique abilities and heritage, and ultimately take their first steps toward ending the devastating war that has consumed their world for nearly two decades.

Main Characters

Clay

MudWing

The main protagonist of the book. Clay is a MudWing hatched from a blood-red egg, giving him immunity to fire. He's loyal, protective, and often hungry. As a "bigwings" (first hatched in a clutch), he has natural protective instincts toward his friends.

Tsunami

SeaWing

A strong-willed and sometimes impulsive SeaWing princess. Daughter of Queen Coral, Tsunami is a skilled fighter who dreams of seeing the ocean. She's brave, determined, and often takes charge of situations.

Glory

RainWing

A RainWing who replaced the SkyWing egg in the prophecy. Often underestimated and mistreated by the guardians, Glory is intelligent, sarcastic, and determined to prove her worth. She can change her scale colors and spit deadly venom.

Starflight

NightWing

A scholarly NightWing who loves scrolls and knowledge. Starflight is intelligent but often anxious and indecisive. He lacks the mind-reading and prophetic abilities most NightWings claim to have.

Sunny

SandWing (Hybrid)

A small, golden SandWing who lacks the venomous tail barb typical of her tribe. Sunny is optimistic, kind, and often underestimated. Later revealed to be half NightWing, explaining her unusual appearance.

World & Setting

The story takes place on Pyrrhia, a dragon-shaped continent divided into territories ruled by seven dragon tribes. Each tribe has unique abilities and characteristics adapted to their environments.

MudWings

Live in the Mud Kingdom in swampy wetlands. They have thick, armored scales and can hold their breath for up to an hour. MudWings hatched from blood-red eggs are immune to fire.

SandWings

Inhabit the Kingdom of Sand, a vast desert. They have venomous barbed tails, can survive long periods without water, and their scales radiate heat.

SeaWings

Rule the Kingdom of the Sea and its islands. They can breathe underwater, see in the dark, and have bioluminescent scales used for communication underwater.

RainWings

Live in the Rainforest Kingdom. They can change their scale colors at will, spit deadly venom, and have prehensile tails for swinging through trees.

NightWings

Originally from a volcanic island, now in the rainforest. They can blend into shadows and supposedly have mind-reading and prophetic abilities (though this is later revealed to be mostly false).

SkyWings

Inhabit the Sky Kingdom in the mountains. They're the fastest flyers with the largest wings and can breathe fire.

IceWings

Live in the Ice Kingdom in the far north. They have serrated claws, can withstand extreme cold, and breathe deadly frostbreath that freezes targets.

Themes & Messages

Peace in a War-Torn World

The central theme revolves around the struggle to achieve peace in a world consumed by war. The dragonets are raised with the purpose of ending the War of SandWing Succession.

Friendship & Unity

The five dragonets form a strong bond despite coming from different tribes that are traditionally enemies. Their friendship transcends tribal boundaries and prejudices.

Destiny vs. Free Will

The dragonets struggle with the weight of their predetermined destiny as dictated by the prophecy, eventually questioning whether they must follow it or forge their own path.

Truth & Deception

The dragonets discover that much of what they were taught was lies or half-truths, including the prophecy itself, which was fabricated to manipulate them.
